# Pitch Script — Exact Words

> Target: 2:55 spoken. 5 seconds of buffer for transitions.
> Delivery: confident, slow on hooks, fast on transitions. **Pause after every period in the hooks.**
> All English. Luis on stage. Single-screen demo behind.

---

## 0:00 — Hook (15s)

> Good morning.
>
> In 2030, you won't convince humans to choose your hotel.
>
> You'll convince their agents.
>
> **Agents are the new SEO. And Rosewood — Rosewood is where agents bring their humans.**

*[Pause. Click slide.]*

## 0:15 — Problem (25s)

> Today, hotels run on ERPs. Today, guests run on apps.
>
> By 2030, both are dead.
>
> Hotels will run on agentic platforms. Guests will run on personal agents — Claude, ChatGPT, the AI assistant on every phone.
>
> The question isn't whether this happens. It's already happening.
>
> The question is: **how does the guest's agent talk to the hotel?**
>
> Today, it can't.

## 0:40 — Introduce HAP (15s)

> Anthropic built MCP — Model Context Protocol — to connect models to the world.
>
> We propose **HAP — Hospitality Agent Protocol** — to connect guests to hospitality.
>
> Open. Auditable. Zero retention. Let me show you.

*[Switch to demo layout. Hands on keyboard.]*

## 0:55 — Demo #1: The Handshake (65s)

*[Luis types in Claude:]*
> *"Voy a Rosewood Sand Hill el 18 de mayo, tengo reuniones en Sand Hill Road."*

*[Consent Checklist appears. Luis narrates:]*

> What you see on the left is my Claude. It just discovered Rosewood's HAP server. Before sharing anything, it asks me what to authorize. **Scope by scope. Time-bound. Auditable.**

*[Luis uncheks "Health context", clicks Approve.]*

> I just sent six categories of preference to Rosewood — for seventy-two hours.

*[Right side fires. Audit log streams. Luis points at it.]*

> Watch the right side. The hotel's concierge agent is **classifying me into a Bleisure profile** — corporate Monday to Wednesday, leisure Thursday to Saturday. It's loading the Sense of Place layer for Sand Hill. And it's generating an arrival brief — for staff, not for me.

*[Staff brief materializes. ElevenLabs voice plays.]*

> *"Welcome ahead of time, Luis. Your room awaits at the temperature of an autumn evening, with matcha from Uji. Wednesday afternoon, the southern patio is yours alone."*

> That voice is ElevenLabs Conversational. The brief on screen — that goes to my room attendant. **The guest never asked for any of this.**

## 2:00 — Demo #2: Reputation Defense (30s)

*[Pivot to Reputation Audit panel.]*

> Now — HAP isn't only for guests. It's also for Rosewood.
>
> Watch what happens when a bad review hits.

*[Click: Simulate Tripadvisor 2-star review.]*
*[A fake angry review appears: "Terrible AC, worst service."]*
*[Click: Generate dispute brief.]*
*[Cryptographically signed brief renders: timeline, dual escalation, 11-minute resolution.]*

> The system has a signed, immutable timeline of every interaction. The AC was fixed in eleven minutes. Two humans were dispatched. The guest left satisfied.
>
> This brief saves the General Manager four hours every time a review like this lands. And gives Rosewood real standing with TrustYou, Tripadvisor, Google.
>
> **That's ROI in week one.**

## 2:30 — Show the Scope (20s)

*[Click to Slide 4 — 10 profiles grid.]*

> HAP covers ten guest profiles. Bleisure. Wellness. Family with minors. Compassionate. VIP-Discrete. Each with operational rules.
>
> The most important: **complaints always escalate to a human. Children always check in with a human. The AI augments staff. Never replaces them.**
>
> That's how ultra-luxury survives the agentic era.

## 2:50 — Close (10s)

*[Click to Slide 5 — three lines visible.]*

> Open protocol. Reference implementation. **Rosewood defines the category.**
>
> HAP is on GitHub today. Thank you.

*[Smile. Look up. Wait for Q&A.]*
